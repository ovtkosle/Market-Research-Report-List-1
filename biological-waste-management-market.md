<p><p>Biological waste management involves various methods to safely treat and dispose of hazardous biological materials. Incineration is a thermal destruction process that reduces waste volume and eliminates pathogens through high-temperature combustion. Autoclaving utilizes steam and pressure to sterilize waste, making it safe for disposal or recycling. Other methods may include chemical disinfection, microwaving, or mechanical processes, each offering unique advantages depending on the type of biological waste and regulatory requirements. Together, these methods ensure effective and safe waste management.</p></p>
