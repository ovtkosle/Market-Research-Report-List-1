<p><p>The emission control catalyst market for small engines is divided into two primary types: diesel-based and gasoline-based emission catalysts. Diesel-based emission catalysts focus on reducing harmful emissions from diesel engines through technologies like selective catalytic reduction (SCR) and diesel oxidation catalysts (DOC). In contrast, gasoline-based emission catalysts, primarily catalytic converters, aim to minimize pollutants from gasoline engines, utilizing three-way catalysts to convert harmful gases into harmless substances. Both types play crucial roles in meeting regulatory standards and improving air quality.</p></p>
