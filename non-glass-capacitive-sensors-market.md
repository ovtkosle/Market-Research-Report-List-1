<p><p>Non-glass capacitive sensors are categorized into three main types based on their dielectric materials: plastic, polymer, and sapphire. Plastic capacitive sensors are lightweight and cost-effective, suitable for various applications. Polymer capacitive sensors offer flexibility and durability, making them ideal for harsh environments. Sapphire capacitive sensors, known for their exceptional hardness and chemical resistance, are used in demanding applications where reliability is critical. Each type serves distinct industries, balancing factors like cost, performance, and environmental resilience.</p></p>
