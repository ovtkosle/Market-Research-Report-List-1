<p><p>PPTC (Polymeric Positive Temperature Coefficient) and CPTC (Ceramic Positive Temperature Coefficient) are types of thermistors used for overcurrent protection in electrical circuits. PPTCs are made from polymer materials that exhibit a dramatic increase in resistance when a certain temperature is reached, thereby limiting current flow. CPTCs, on the other hand, utilize ceramic materials and also enhance resistance upon heating. Both types serve essential roles in managing electrical safety, supporting diverse applications in consumer electronics, automotive, and industrial sectors.</p></p>
