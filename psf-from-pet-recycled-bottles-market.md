<p><p>PSF, or Polyester Staple Fiber, derived from recycled PET bottles is categorized into mechanical and chemical markets. The mechanical market involves the physical recycling process, where discarded bottles are cleaned, shredded, and processed into fiber. This method is cost-effective and widely used for textiles and non-woven applications. In contrast, the chemical market utilizes advanced methods to break down PET into its monomers, allowing for the production of high-quality fibers. This approach is ideal for applications requiring superior properties and durability.</p></p>
