<p><ul><li>Non-Emulsion Type</li><li>Emulsion Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The all-vegetable shortening market can be divided into two primary types: non-emulsion type and emulsion type. Non-emulsion shortening is a solid fat that enhances texture and stability in baked goods, providing high plasticity and resistance to separation. In contrast, emulsion type shortening includes water and other emulsifiers, resulting in a creamy texture that improves moisture retention and creates lighter baked products. Both types are essential for various culinary applications, each serving distinct roles in achieving desired product characteristics.</p></p>
