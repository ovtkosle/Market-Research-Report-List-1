<p><p>The oral thin film drugs market includes two main types: sublingual films and fully dissolving dental/buccal films. Sublingual films are designed to dissolve under the tongue, allowing for rapid drug absorption into the bloodstream, ideal for medications requiring quick effects. Fully dissolving dental or buccal films adhere to the inner cheek, providing a sustained release of medication while enhancing patient compliance. Both film types offer advantages such as ease of use, discreet administration, and improved bioavailability compared to traditional tablets.</p></p>
