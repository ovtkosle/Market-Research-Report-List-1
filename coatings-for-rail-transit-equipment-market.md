<p><p>The coatings for rail transit equipment encompass various types that serve distinct functions. Primers are foundational layers that enhance adhesion and corrosion resistance. Putty is used for filling imperfections and surface preparation. Medium paints provide color and protective properties, often offering a balance between aesthetics and durability. Topcoats deliver a final protective layer that can resist environmental factors like UV rays and moisture. Damping paints reduce vibration and noise, improving passenger comfort and equipment longevity. Each type plays a crucial role in maintaining rail infrastructure.</p></p>
